00 — The Three Mandates
Mandate I Build the proof before the pitch.
No cold outreach without visible work. The Notion showcase, the Instagram feed, and the website must exist before Bureau speaks to anyone.
Mandate II Start local. Scale without limits.
Kolkata is the anchor. The first clients, the first case studies, and the first trust are built here — then the work travels.
Mandate III Let the work do the talking.
Bureau's marketing is Bureau's work — the feed, the documents, the templates. Every output is a portfolio piece before it is a product.
01 — Ideal Client Profile
I Primary Profile · Launch Phase The Ambitious Founder

Early-stage business owners and solo operators in Kolkata — running real businesses but doing everything manually. They have the ambition but not the infrastructure. Systems are the entry point; Signal and Front follow naturally.

  • 0–5 years in business, operating without formal systems
  • Relying on WhatsApp, notebooks, or scattered spreadsheets
  • Aware their brand presence doesn't match their actual quality
  • Budget-conscious but willing to invest when value is clear
  • Likely in: consulting, coaching, F&B, retail, creative services
  • Referral-driven — one good client becomes three
II Secondary Profile · Online Reach The Notion-Curious Professional

Freelancers, consultants, and knowledge workers anywhere in India who have discovered Notion but can't get it to work for them. They find Bureau through templates, content, and the Notion community — and may never meet us in person.

  • Remote-first, digitally fluent, India-wide
  • Already trying to self-build their operating system
  • Willing to pay for a template that saves them weeks of work
  • Entry via Blueprints; upsell path to Bespoke is natural
  • Likely in: design, writing, development, education, finance
  • Feeds Bureau's passive income and national reputation
02 — Launch Phases
I Month 1 — Foundation Build the proof.
Before Bureau speaks to anyone, Bureau must have something to show. This phase is entirely production — no outreach.
Publish the Bureau website on Wix — hero, three services, contact form
Launch the showcase Notion workspace — publicly shareable, visually polished
Open Bureau Instagram — post 6 foundational content pieces before the first follow request
Create and list the first two Blueprints templates — priced to sell
Set up the Bureau LinkedIn page with positioning statement and first post
Define the intake process — how a lead becomes a brief becomes a project
II Months 2–3 — First Clients Earn the first three.
Outreach begins — but carefully. The first three clients are chosen, not acquired. They become Bureau's proof of concept.
Identify 3 businesses in Kolkata personally — warm connections or observed gaps
Offer a Bureau Systems audit — free initial conversation, paid engagement if there's fit
Deliver at an unusually high standard — the quality of the work is the strategy
Ask for one referral from each satisfied client — not a testimonial, a referral
Begin posting on Instagram consistently — 3 posts per week minimum
Join and contribute to the Notion Creators community — presence, not promotion
III Months 4–6 — Compounding Let it compound.
By this point, Bureau has case studies, a live feed, and a reputation beginning to form. The goal is to systematise what's working.
Publish the first Bureau case study — Notion workspace before/after, documented
Expand Blueprints to 4–6 templates across different use cases
Introduce Bureau Signal to existing Systems clients — natural conversation
Begin LinkedIn long-form content — 1 article per fortnight on operations & brand
Reassess pricing — raise Bespoke floor if demand justifies it
Target a second city — Mumbai or Bengaluru — for one Bespoke engagement

06 — Pricing Rationale
Why these prices, in Kolkata.

Bureau's pricing is calibrated to the Kolkata market — competitive enough to remove the cost objection, high enough to signal that this is not commodity work. The goal in the launch phase is not maximum revenue. It is maximum proof.

  • Blueprints at ₹499–₹2,999 create zero-friction entry points
  • Bespoke at ₹8,000–₹25,000 is accessible to serious founders
  • Prices are reviewed upward after the first three case studies
  • Signal and Front are positioned as premium — always
The upsell architecture.

Bureau is not designed for one-time transactions. Every engagement is structured to open the next door. A client who buys a Blueprint is shown what Bespoke looks like. A client with a Bespoke OS is asked if their brand reflects it.

  • Blueprint → Bespoke: Show the gap between template and custom
  • Systems → Signal: Ask if the brand matches the operation
  • Signal → Front: Ask if the website matches the brand
  • All three: The complete Bureau client — the long-term goal
